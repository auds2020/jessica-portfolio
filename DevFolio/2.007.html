Over the course of the semester, we learn various engineering and physics principles to gain a more comprehensive understanding of design mechanisms. To gain hands-on experience and utilize the knowledge from class, we are given a field with various challenges and tasked with designing and fabricating a robot to complete these.

The challenge

The game field included several tasks (where bolded tasks are):

  • Lifting the beaver to 12, 24, or 40 in
  • Press buttons to release balls into the pachinko board
  • collect and deposit balls into the pachinko board
  • Pull on a ring
  • Tip the street lamp
  • Drop square peg into round hole

    • My goals for my robot included:

  • Using only two motors
  • Driving up and down a 25-degree slope to do tasks on a top and bottom level

    • My robot

    To minimize overall robot dimension to meet starting size regulation, I designed mechanisms that can be used to score multiple tasks. Kaplinko is made of 3 main modules the chassis, the lift mechanism, and hook attachment.

    Issues with V1:

  • Belt required more than 1” height clearance
  • Everything needed to be machined by hand (time-consuming and higher error)
  • The dimensions of the extrusions are set and access to different size extrusions very limited

    • Chassis

    The chassis is a four wheel belt tank drive to maximize traction. This was important in order drive up and down the 25 degree slope and to pull on the ring. The system is powered with two motors which are on opposite sides to decrease overall robot width. The chassis is made from laser cut and bent sheet metal to increase precision and customization. The top of the chassis had a hole pattern to easily mount, test, and iterate on other modules.

    Lift

    The main goals for the lift mechanism was to raise the 5kg beaver up 40 inches, press the bullseye buttons, and tip the street car lamp. Some constraints imposed was the robot cannot exceed 16” in its starting position and access to limited motor selections.

    The lift is made from aluminum box extrusions which are connected by a Delrin roller assembles that slide between adjacent extrusions. A string, controller by a motor, transfers motion through tension and pulleys to raise or lower the lift. Attached to the end of the lift is a platform to push up the beaver and press buttons and sheet metal to pull and tip the street lamp. Force and motor calculations were used to determine the optimal size of the spool the string wraps around to lift the beaver as quickly as possible.

    Hook

    The hook is a laser-cut aluminum piece controlled by a servo. It secures the robot to the ring before driving forward, pulling the ring out.
