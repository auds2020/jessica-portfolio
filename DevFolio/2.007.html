The challenge

My goals for my robot included:

  • Using only two motors
  • Driving up and down a 25-degree slope to do tasks on a top and bottom level

    • Powered by two motors Left and Right sides are assembled such that the motors are on opposite sides of the chassis to decrease overall width. Top plate with holes to make the system more modular

    V1 Chassis

    Aluminum Extrusion Modules The Left and Right Modules are made from Aluminum Extrusion - One square + One L piece for each side module Laser cut sheet metal for the top plate with equally spaced holes to easily integrate other systems

    Issues with V1:

  • Belt required more than 1” height clearance
  • Everything needed to be machined by hand (time-consuming and higher error)
  • The dimensions of the extrusions are set and access to different size extrusions very limited

    • V2 Chassis

    Switched out the Aluminum extrusion for laser cut sheet metal that was stacked and bent, which had 2 benefits:

  • Provided additional flexibility for location of holes placement
  • Easily and quickly make changes and swap out pieces

    • Kept the top plate with equally spaced holes to attach
